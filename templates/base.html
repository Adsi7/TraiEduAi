WHAT ARE TRANSFERABLE SKILLS?

Transferable skills refer to a broad range of abilities encompassing cognitive, emotional and social competencies. These skills can be applied across different contexts and are not limited to specific tasks or industries. They empower individuals to navigate diverse challenges, communicate effectively, solve problems, and build strong relationships, fostering personal and professional growth.

Cognitive Skills

Creativity

Ability to generate original ideas, solutions, and concepts, to think outside the box, take risks and challenge conventional thinking; helps to innovate, solve problems, and achieve success in a variety of fields.

Organization

Ability to manage one's own time, tasks, and priorities in an effective and efficient manner, without external supervision or direction. It involves setting goals, developing plans, prioritizing tasks, and managing one's own workload in order to meet deadlines and achieve objectives.

Critical Reasoning

Ability to analyze information, evaluate arguments, and draw logical conclusions. It requires individuals to use their analytical skills, judgment, and reasoning abilities to identify the strengths and weaknesses of arguments and make informed decisions based on evidence and reasoning

Social Skills

Communication

Ability to effectively convey information through written, verbal, and nonverbal means. It is crucial in all aspects of life, including personal relationships, work, and social situations. Thus it is important because it helps to build trust, avoid misunderstandings, and foster positive relationships.

Leadership

Ability to inspire, motivate, and guide others towards a common goal, to communicate effectively, delegate tasks, and make decisions in a fair and ethical manner. It is important because it helps to create a positive work environment, increase productivity, and achieve organizational goals.

Empathy

Ability to understand and share the feelings and perspectives of others. It involves being able to put yourself in someone else's shoes and see things from their point of view, as well as being able to identify and understand their emotions.

Emotional Skills

Resilience

Ability to adapt and bounce back from adversity , whether in personal or professional life. It involves a combination of mental, emotional, and behavioral skills that enable individuals to cope with stress.

Emotional Regulation

Ability to manage and control one's own emotions in a healthy and productive way. It involves recognizing and understanding one's own emotions, and being able to respond to them in a way that is appropriate and adaptive to the situation.
